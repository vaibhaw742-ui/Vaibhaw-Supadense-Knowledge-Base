Here's my new view on AI agents.

---

## Horse carriages

Humans used to push carts ourselves.

The cart did the carrying, and we did the pushing.

Then we found horses. Far stronger than us at pulling.

We put a harness on the horse, so it could be connected to the cart and we could steer.

The horse pulled the cart far better than we ever could. We didn't have to push anymore.

That became the carriage.

---

## AI agents

Humans used to operate computers ourselves.

The computer did the computing, and we did the operating.

Then we found LLMs. Far smarter and faster than us at many cognitive operations: reading, writing, planning, translating intent into steps, and working across interfaces.

We put a harness on the AI, so it could be connected to the computer and we could steer.

The AI operated the computer far better than we ever could. At a speed, scale, and persistence we never could. We didn't have to operate anymore.

That is what we call an AI agent.

![Image](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HHeowplaIAAO_kB?format=jpg&name=small)

===

For context: people usually describe AI agents as "we give AI the power to use tools." I think it's the other way around. The tools (computers) were always the powerful thing. The AI is the new operator.
